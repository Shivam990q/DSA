# 📖 The Complete Library

> *"Stand on the shoulders of giants — they wrote it all down."*

Curated resources for every section of this codex. Prioritize the ⭐ items.

---

## I. FOUNDATIONS & GENERAL PL

- ⭐ **Programming Language Pragmatics** — Michael L. Scott. The best single-volume survey of how languages work (syntax, semantics, implementation). Start here.
- ⭐ **Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP)** — Abelson & Sussman. Free online (MIT). Reshapes how you think; builds interpreters in Scheme.
- **Concepts, Techniques, and Models of Computer Programming (CTM)** — Van Roy & Haridi. Deep multi-paradigm treatment.
- **Seven Languages in Seven Weeks** — Bruce Tate. A polyglot tour to stretch your thinking.
- **Practical Foundations for Programming Languages (PFPL)** — Robert Harper. Rigorous, type-theoretic foundations.

---

## II. COMPILERS & INTERPRETERS

- ⭐ **Crafting Interpreters** — Bob Nystrom. Free online. Builds a tree-walking interpreter *and* a bytecode VM for a real language. The best hands-on book — do this after the [language-design section](../10-LANGUAGE-DESIGN/00-Index.md).
- ⭐ **Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools** ("The Dragon Book") — Aho, Lam, Sethi, Ullman. The classic compiler reference.
- **Writing an Interpreter in Go** / **Writing a Compiler in Go** — Thorsten Ball. Approachable, practical.
- **Engineering a Compiler** — Cooper & Torczon. Modern, optimization-focused.
- **Modern Compiler Implementation in ML/C/Java** — Andrew Appel. The "Tiger books."
- **LLVM documentation & Kaleidoscope tutorial** — build a real compiler front end on LLVM.

---

## III. TYPE SYSTEMS & THEORY

- ⭐ **Types and Programming Languages (TAPL)** — Benjamin C. Pierce. The type-theory bible. Rigorous but readable.
- **Advanced Topics in Types and Programming Languages (ATTAPL)** — Pierce (ed.). The follow-up for advanced topics.
- **Software Foundations** — Pierce et al. Free, interactive, in Coq — learn logic, types, and proofs by doing (Curry–Howard live).
- **Type Theory and Formal Proof** — Nederpelt & Geuvers. For the dependent-types road.
- **Homotopy Type Theory (HoTT Book)** — free; the frontier of type theory as a foundation of math.

---

## IV. FUNCTIONAL PROGRAMMING & LAMBDA CALCULUS

- ⭐ **Learn You a Haskell for Great Good** — Miran Lipovača. Free, gentle, fun intro to Haskell.
- **Programming in Haskell** — Graham Hutton. Concise and excellent.
- **Structure and Interpretation** (SICP, again) — the functional classic.
- **An Introduction to Functional Programming Through Lambda Calculus** — Greg Michaelson. Exactly what it says.
- **The Little Schemer** — Friedman & Felleisen. Recursion and thinking, Socratically.

---

## V. MEMORY, RUNTIMES & CONCURRENCY

- **The Garbage Collection Handbook** — Jones, Hosking, Moss. The definitive GC reference.
- ⭐ **The Rust Programming Language** ("The Book") — Klabnik & Nichols. Free online. The best intro to ownership.
- **Programming Rust** — Blandy, Orendorff, Tindall. Deeper, systems-focused.
- **The Art of Multiprocessor Programming** — Herlihy & Shavit. The concurrency classic.
- **Java Concurrency in Practice** — Goetz et al. Even beyond Java, the best treatment of threads/memory models.
- **Seven Concurrency Models in Seven Weeks** — Paul Butcher. Threads, actors, CSP, STM, data parallelism — a tour.

---

## VI. LANGUAGE-SPECIFIC DEEP DIVES

- **C** — *The C Programming Language* (K&R); *Expert C Programming* (van der Linden).
- **C++** — *Effective C++* / *Effective Modern C++* (Scott Meyers); *The C++ Programming Language* (Stroustrup).
- **Rust** — *The Rust Book* (free); *Rust for Rustaceans* (Jon Gjengset).
- **Go** — *The Go Programming Language* (Donovan & Kernighan).
- **Java** — *Effective Java* (Joshua Bloch) — essential.
- **Python** — *Fluent Python* (Luciano Ramalho) — the deep one.
- **JavaScript** — *You Don't Know JS Yet* (Kyle Simpson, free); *Eloquent JavaScript* (free).
- **Haskell** — *Learn You a Haskell* (free); *Real World Haskell* (free).

## VII. COURSES

- **CMU 15-150** (Functional Programming), **15-312/15-814** (Principles of Programming Languages / Types).
- **Stanford CS143** (Compilers) — and the classic Coursera Compilers course (Alex Aiken).
- **MIT 6.001** (SICP) — legendary, lectures free online.
- **University of Washington CSE 341** (Programming Languages) — Dan Grossman; also on Coursera.
- **Oregon Programming Languages Summer School (OPLSS)** — free lectures at the research frontier.

---

## VIII. PAPERS & ONGOING

- **"Go To Statement Considered Harmful"** — Dijkstra (1968). Structured programming.
- **"Recursive Functions of Symbolic Expressions"** — McCarthy (1960). Lisp's genesis.
- **"On Understanding Types, Data Abstraction, and Polymorphism"** — Cardelli & Wegner (1985).
- **"Propositions as Types"** — Philip Wadler. The clearest exposition of Curry–Howard. ⭐
- **Conferences to follow:** POPL, PLDI, ICFP, OOPSLA/SPLASH — the PL research frontier.
- **Sites:** [cs.brown.edu/~sk/Publications/Books/ProgLangs/](https://www.plai.org) (PLAI, free), [Papers We Love](https://paperswelove.org).

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways
- Build + read in parallel: **Crafting Interpreters** (build) + **TAPL/SICP** (theory).
- **Programming Language Pragmatics** is the best single survey; the **Dragon Book** for compilers; **TAPL** for types.
- Every language has a canonical deep-dive (Effective Java, Fluent Python, The Rust Book, K&R).
- Follow POPL/PLDI/ICFP and "Propositions as Types" for the frontier.
