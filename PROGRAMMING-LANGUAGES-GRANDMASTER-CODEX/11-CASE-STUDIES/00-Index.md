# 📚 Case Studies — Learning from History's Decisions

> *"Every language is a frozen argument. Study the arguments and you learn design."*

Real languages made real decisions with real consequences. This section dissects a few legendary ones — the mistakes that cost billions, the constraints that shaped winners, and the bold bets that paid off.

## 🧭 Why Case Studies

Principles ([`../10-LANGUAGE-DESIGN/01-How-to-Design-a-Language.md`](../10-LANGUAGE-DESIGN/01-How-to-Design-a-Language.md)) are abstract until you see them play out. These stories make the tradeoffs visceral: what happens when you add `null`, when you break backward compatibility, when you refuse features, when you ship in a hurry. History is the best design teacher.

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways
- Language design decisions have decades-long, sometimes billion-dollar consequences.
- Studying real outcomes turns abstract principles into engineering judgment.
