This level has no curriculum. It has a **way of being**.

You wake. You think. You read a paper. You wonder. You write on a notebook. You implement. You stress-test. You discover something. You publish. You teach.

You are no longer a student. You are now a **discoverer**.

## 🪞 THE FIVE ARCHETYPES

Pick (or merge) the path that fits you:

### 🪐 Archetype 1: The Theoretician
You spend your life proving lower bounds, identifying open problems, and producing algorithms with elegant guarantees.
**Heroes**: Avi Wigderson, Madhu Sudan, Robert Tarjan.

### 🚀 Archetype 2: The Engineer-Researcher
You take theoretical algorithms and make them ship at planet-scale (Google Maps, Stripe, Uber).
**Heroes**: Jeff Dean, Sanjay Ghemawat, Robert Bowdidge.

### 🎓 Archetype 3: The Educator
You write books, lecture, train students. Your impact is measured in disciples.
**Heroes**: Donald Knuth, Robert Sedgewick, Tim Roughgarden.

### ⚔️ Archetype 4: The Problem Setter
You craft legendary problems that rest in IOI / ICPC / CF archives forever, teaching future generations.
**Heroes**: Mike Mirzayanov (CF), Hisoka ([AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp) writers), Andrew Stankevich (legendary ICPC writer).

### 🧬 Archetype 5: The Inter-Disciplinary
You apply algorithm thinking to biology, physics, economics, climate. You solve civilization-scale problems with algorithmic tools.
**Heroes**: Lov Grover (quantum), Manolis Kellis (computational biology), David Donoho (compressed sensing).

## 🔮 PRACTICES OF THE GRANDMASTER

### Practice 1: **Daily Pondering** (Always-On)
Some problem from your specialty is in the back of your mind 24/7. Walking, showering, sleeping. The mind chews. Insights come unbidden.

### Practice 2: **Notebook Discipline**
You carry a physical notebook. Every idea, no matter how wild, gets written. Most are wrong. The few that are right change the field.

### Practice 3: **Reading 2-3 papers/week**
Not casually. Deeply. You re-derive proofs. You implement. You critique.

### Practice 4: **Teaching**
You teach — even informally. Even a single student. Teaching is the greatest debugger of one's own understanding.

### Practice 5: **Open-source Mastery**
Your algorithms exist in code. They are downloaded, used, tested by adversaries. The world catches your bugs. You catch your assumptions.

### Practice 6: **Cross-pollination**
You read outside your field. Mathematics, physics, biology, economics. The deepest algorithms steal ideas from neighboring fields.

### Practice 7: **Mentorship Network**
You have 1-2 peers at your level. You exchange ideas weekly. You have 5-10 students you mentor. You have 1-2 mentors who are 10 years ahead.

## 🎯 THE TEN HARDEST PROBLEMS YOU MIGHT TACKLE

(Choose your battle. These are humanity's frontier.)

1. **P vs NP** — Worth $1M (Clay Prize) and immortality.
2. **Graph isomorphism in P** — Babai got close (quasipolynomial); P remains open.
3. **Online MST in deterministic O(log² n)** per update — open.
4. **Faster matrix multiplication** — current is O(n^2.371) (December 2023). Lower bound is Ω(n²). Gap: open.
5. **Polynomial-time max-flow** — recent breakthroughs (Chen et al.) achieved O(m^(1+o(1))). Best deterministic still gap.
6. **Strong exponential time hypothesis** — proving or refuting it.
7. **Faster shortest paths with negative weights** — recent O(m log² n) (Bernstein-Nanongkai-Saranurak), but constants/practicality open.
8. **3SUM lower bound** — strongly believed Ω(n²) but unproven.
9. **Halting problem variants for restricted Turing machines** — many open.
10. **Quantum supremacy on practical problems** — engineering + algorithm frontier.
