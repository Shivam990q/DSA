# ⚔️ Codeforces Round Simulation

> *"2.5 hours. 5-6 problems. Your rating on the line."*

---

## I. THE FORMAT
- Div 2: 2-2.5 hours, problems A-F (increasing difficulty)
- Rated; performance determines rating change

---

## II. SIMULATE A VIRTUAL ROUND
- Pick a past Div 2 you haven't seen (Codeforces → Gym → virtual participation)
- Set 2.5h timer
- No editorial, no help
- Treat as live (rating pressure mindset)

---

## III. THE TIME PLAN
```
Min 0-3:    Read all problems (A-F)
Min 3-10:   Solve A (warmup, fast)
Min 10-25:  Solve B
Min 25-60:  Solve C (the differentiator)
Min 60-100: Attempt D
Min 100-150: Attempt E/F if D done
Final 10:   Verify submissions; stress-test if uncertain
```

---

## IV. THE A-B-C-D PROGRESSION
- **A**: usually trivial (implementation/observation). Solve in <10 min.
- **B**: easy (greedy/math/sorting). <15 min.
- **C**: medium (the rating mover). Spend deeply.
- **D**: harder (DP/graphs/data structures). Climb if time.
- **E/F**: hard (only if you've cleared the rest).

---

## V. THE MINDSET RULES
- Don't lock into one problem >40 min without progress. Switch.
- Don't submit untested code (penalty + WA hurts).
- Read C carefully — it's where most rating is won/lost at Expert level.

---

## VI. POST-ROUND
- Note solved/unsolved
- Upsolve unsolved (no editorial for 30 min first)
- Read editorial; re-implement
- Log patterns + mistakes

---

## VII. RATING REALITY
- Rating reflects your TRUE skill over many contests (variance averages out)
- One bad round ≠ your skill
- Goal: 50+ rated rounds before judging your "real" rating

---

## VIII. RESOURCES
- **Codeforces** → past rounds → virtual participation
- **A2OJ ladders** for structured difficulty progression
- **CF Predictor** browser extension (live rating estimate)
