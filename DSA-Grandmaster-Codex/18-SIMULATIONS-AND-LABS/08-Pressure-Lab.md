# 🔥 Pressure Lab — Solving Under Stress

> *"Calm in practice, calm in war. Panic in practice, panic in war."*

---

## I. WHY TRAIN UNDER PRESSURE
You can solve problems calmly at home but freeze in interviews/contests. The skill of performing under pressure is SEPARATE and must be trained.

---

## II. PRESSURE SIMULATIONS

### Lab 1: Timed pressure
Solve a Medium in HALF your usual time. The clock creates urgency.

### Lab 2: Audience pressure
Solve while a friend watches silently. Being observed triggers stress.

### Lab 3: Narration pressure
Solve while explaining aloud (record). Dual-tasking taxes working memory.

### Lab 4: Noise pressure
Solve in a noisy café / with background distraction. Trains focus under chaos.

### Lab 5: Stakes pressure
Bet something (push-ups, money to charity) on solving in time. Real consequences.

### Lab 6: Cold-start pressure
Open a random Hard with zero warmup. Mimics the "first problem" jolt.

---

## III. THE STRESS-RESPONSE TRAINING
When stress hits:
1. **Notice it** (racing heart, tunnel vision)
2. **Breathe** (4 seconds in, 4 hold, 4 out)
3. **Reframe** ("this is excitement, not fear")
4. **Anchor** (return to the protocol: read, restate, brute, optimize)

Practice this response repeatedly until it's automatic.

---

## IV. THE PROTOCOL AS ANCHOR
Under pressure, the mind scatters. The PROTOCOL gives it a rail to grip:
```
Read → Restate → Constraints → Brute → Bottleneck → Optimize → Test → Submit
```
When panicking, just execute the next protocol step. Don't try to "be brilliant." Just follow the steps.

---

## V. PHYSIOLOGICAL TOOLS
- **Box breathing** (4-4-4-4) before and during
- **Posture**: sit up; power posture reduces cortisol
- **Hydration**: dehydration amplifies stress
- **Sleep**: tired brain panics easier

---

## VI. THE GRADUATED EXPOSURE
Like exposure therapy, increase pressure gradually:
1. Week 1: timed solo
2. Week 2: timed + narrated
3. Week 3: timed + narrated + observed (mock)
4. Week 4: live contests / real interviews

Each step builds tolerance.

---

## VII. POST-LAB REFLECTION
```
Did I panic? When?
What physical signs appeared?
Did I return to the protocol?
What triggered the stress?
How can I anchor better next time?
```
