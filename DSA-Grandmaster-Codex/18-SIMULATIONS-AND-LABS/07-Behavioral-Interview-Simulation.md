# 🗣️ Behavioral Interview Simulation

> *"They've seen you can code. Now they ask: can we work with you for years?"*

---

## I. THE FORMAT
- 45 minutes
- 5-7 questions about past experiences
- Evaluated on: leadership, collaboration, ownership, conflict resolution, culture fit

---

## II. THE STAR METHOD
- **S**ituation: brief context
- **T**ask: your responsibility
- **A**ction: what YOU did (specific, "I" not "we")
- **R**esult: outcome, ideally quantified

Each story: 2-3 minutes.

---

## III. THE 10 STORIES TO PREPARE
1. Most challenging technical problem
2. Conflict with a teammate
3. Project failure / lesson learned
4. Project that exceeded expectations
5. Taking initiative without being asked
6. Disagreement with manager
7. Tight deadline + ambiguity
8. Mentoring someone
9. Technical decision with tradeoffs
10. Going above and beyond

---

## IV. SIMULATE
- Have a friend ask random behavioral questions (or self-record)
- Answer in STAR format, 2-3 min each
- Watch playback for: rambling, vagueness, "we" overuse, lack of result

---

## V. COMPANY-SPECIFIC MAPPING
- **Amazon**: map stories to the 16 Leadership Principles (2-3 per LP)
- **Google**: collaboration, ambiguity, learning ("Googliness")
- **Meta**: move fast, impact, boldness
- **Microsoft/Apple**: growth mindset, customer focus

---

## VI. EVALUATION RUBRIC
```
☐ Used STAR structure
☐ Specific (not abstract)
☐ "I" not "we" (own your contribution)
☐ Quantified results where possible
☐ Showed self-awareness / growth
☐ Stayed within 2-3 min
☐ Mapped to company values
```

---

## VII. COMMON MISTAKES
- Vague, generic answers
- Taking credit for team work ("we")
- No clear result
- Rambling (>4 min)
- Negativity about past employers
- No reflection/learning

---

## VIII. RESOURCES
- Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles (official)
- "Cracking the PM/Coding Interview" behavioral sections
- Practice with friends in industry
