# 🥇 Teaching Trial Bank

> *"You don't truly understand something until you can make a confused person understand it."*

For each concept, explain it to a smart but uninformed person (a friend, a rubber duck, a camera) in the time limit. They (or you, on playback) must genuinely understand.

---

## THE TEACHING TEST FORMAT
- Explain WITHOUT notes
- Use simple language + an example
- Build from first principles
- Check: would a beginner understand?

---

## LEVEL 0-1 CONCEPTS (5 min each)
- [ ] What is recursion? (3 parts)
- [ ] Why does a hash table give O(1) lookup?
- [ ] What is Big-O notation?
- [ ] How does binary search work, and why O(log n)?
- [ ] What's the difference between a stack and a queue?

## LEVEL 2 CONCEPTS (10 min each)
- [ ] Why does DP need overlapping subproblems?
- [ ] How does Dijkstra's greedy choice work, and why non-negative weights?
- [ ] How does Union-Find achieve near-O(1)?
- [ ] Why is comparison sorting Ω(n log n)?
- [ ] How does a heap maintain its property in O(log n)?

## LEVEL 3-4 CONCEPTS (15 min each)
- [ ] How does a segment tree answer range queries in O(log n)?
- [ ] What is lazy propagation and why does it work?
- [ ] How does KMP avoid re-examining text? (failure function)
- [ ] Why does the monotonic stack solve "next greater" in O(n)?
- [ ] How does an LRU cache achieve O(1)?

## LEVEL 5+ CONCEPTS (15-20 min each)
- [ ] How does FFT multiply polynomials in O(n log n)?
- [ ] How does HLD enable path queries in O(log² n)?
- [ ] How does max-flow min-cut duality work?
- [ ] How does the Aliens trick handle "exactly k" constraints?
- [ ] How does a suffix automaton represent all substrings?

## LEVEL 9-10 CONCEPTS
- [ ] Explain a recent breakthrough paper to a peer
- [ ] Teach a full 1-hour session on an advanced topic
- [ ] Mentor someone through their first hard problem

---

## THE TEACHING RUBRIC
```
☐ Explained from first principles (not memorized facts)
☐ Used a concrete example
☐ Built intuition, not just mechanics
☐ Anticipated and addressed confusion
☐ The listener actually understood (verify!)
☐ Stayed within time
```

---

## WHY TEACHING IS THE ULTIMATE TEST
Teaching forces Stage-4 understanding:
- You can't fake it — gaps become obvious
- You must organize knowledge coherently
- You must anticipate confusion (deep understanding)
- The Feynman technique: if you can't explain simply, you don't understand it

---

## HOW TO PRACTICE
- Explain to a study buddy
- Write a blog post per concept
- Record yourself teaching to a camera
- Answer questions on forums ([Codeforces](https://codeforces.com), Stack Overflow)
- Make a YouTube video
