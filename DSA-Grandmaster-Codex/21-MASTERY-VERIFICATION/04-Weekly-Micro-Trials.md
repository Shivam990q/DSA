# 🔄 Weekly Micro-Trials

> *"Mastery leaks. Plug the leaks weekly."*

---

## I. THE SUNDAY 30-MINUTE SPOT CHECK
```
☐ Pick 3 random algorithms; implement from memory in <10 min each
☐ Pick 1 random LC Medium; solve in <25 min
☐ Pick 1 concept; teach it aloud (record yourself)
☐ Score each: 1 = struggled, 5 = effortless. Goal: 4+ each.
```

If you can't pass micro-trials, you've leaked mastery. Re-cement that week.

---

## II. THE ROTATING ALGORITHM CHECK
Keep a list of your level's core algorithms. Each week, randomly pick 3 to re-implement from memory. Over a month, you cycle through ~12. This catches rust before it compounds.

---

## III. THE RANDOM-PROBLEM CHECK
Pick a random solved problem from 30+ days ago. Can you still solve it quickly? If not, that pattern needs reinforcement.

---

## IV. THE TEACHING CHECK
Pick a concept you "know." Explain it aloud in 5 minutes. Gaps in your explanation = gaps in understanding. Fix them.

---

## V. THE SCORING SYSTEM
| Score | Meaning                          |
|-------|----------------------------------|
| 1     | Couldn't do it                   |
| 2     | Did it with significant struggle |
| 3     | Did it slowly                    |
| 4     | Did it smoothly                  |
| 5     | Effortless, could teach it       |

Target: 4+ on everything at your level. Anything ≤3 → drill it.

---

## VI. THE MONTHLY DEEP CHECK
Once a month, run the full Implementation Trial + a Problem Trial for your level (see [`01-Implementation-Trial-Bank.md`](./01-Implementation-Trial-Bank.md), [`02-Problem-Trial-Bank.md`](./02-Problem-Trial-Bank.md)).

---

## VII. WHY MICRO-TRIALS MATTER
- Knowledge decays without use (the forgetting curve)
- Catching rust early is cheap; catching it late is expensive
- Confidence comes from verified retention, not assumed retention

---

## VIII. THE HABIT
Make the Sunday spot-check part of your weekly review ritual. 30 minutes that protects months of accumulated skill.
