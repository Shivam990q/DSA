# 🔁 Re-Verification Rhythm

> *"Knowledge decays. The grandmaster re-verifies, re-cements, re-sharpens — forever."*

---

## I. THE FORGETTING CURVE
Without reinforcement, memory decays exponentially. A skill learned and never revisited fades. Re-verification fights the forgetting curve.

---

## II. THE RE-VERIFICATION SCHEDULE
| Level    | Re-verify every | Why                                  |
|----------|-----------------|--------------------------------------|
| 0-2      | 3 months        | Foundational; must stay razor-sharp  |
| 3-5      | 6 months        | Interview-relevant; can rust         |
| 6-7      | Continuous      | CP keeps you sharp naturally         |
| 8-10     | Ongoing         | Work/research IS the verification    |

---

## III. THE SPACED-REPETITION PRINCIPLE
Re-solve key problems at increasing intervals:
- Day 0: solve
- Day 7: re-solve (test short-term retention)
- Day 30: re-solve (medium-term)
- Day 90: re-solve (long-term)

Problems you can solve at day 90 are truly internalized.

---

## IV. WHAT TO RE-VERIFY
1. **Core algorithm implementations** (the Implementation Trial Bank)
2. **Pattern recognition** (random problems from past)
3. **Conceptual understanding** (teaching trials)
4. **Speed** (timed re-solves)

---

## V. THE RE-VERIFICATION SESSION (monthly, 2-3 hours)
```
1. Re-implement 5 core algorithms from memory (30 min)
2. Re-solve 3 problems from 30+ days ago (60 min)
3. Teach 2 concepts aloud (30 min)
4. Note what rusted → schedule drills
```

---

## VI. THE LEAK DETECTION
Signs of mastery leak:
- Hesitation on a previously-fluent algorithm
- Forgetting a pattern you "knew"
- Slower solve times
- Inability to explain a concept clearly

Catch these early via weekly micro-trials + monthly re-verification.

---

## VII. THE LIFELONG PRACTICE
Even Level 10 grandmasters re-verify:
- They re-read CLRS annually
- They re-implement classic algorithms
- They teach (which re-verifies)
- They stay in the game

> "Mastery is not a destination you reach and leave. It is a garden you tend forever."

---

## VIII. THE RHYTHM IN PRACTICE
- **Daily**: solve + reflect (natural reinforcement)
- **Weekly**: micro-trial spot check (30 min)
- **Monthly**: re-verification session (2-3 hours)
- **Quarterly**: full level trial re-run
- **Yearly**: re-read philosophy + foundational texts

---

## IX. THE FINAL WORD
> *"The river that stops flowing becomes a swamp.  
>  The skill that stops being used becomes a memory.  
>  Keep flowing. Keep using. Keep verifying.  
>  This is the way."*
