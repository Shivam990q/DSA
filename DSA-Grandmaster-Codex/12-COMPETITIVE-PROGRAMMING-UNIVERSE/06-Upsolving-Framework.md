# 🔄 Upsolving Framework

> *"The contest reveals your gaps. Upsolving fills them. Upsolving > contesting for learning."*

---

## I. WHAT IS UPSOLVING
After a contest, solving the problems you DIDN'T solve during it. This is where 70% of the learning happens.

---

## II. THE UPSOLVE PROTOCOL
```
1. List unsolved problems immediately after the contest
2. For each, attempt WITHOUT editorial for 30-60 min
3. If still stuck, read editorial for the IDEA only
4. Close editorial, re-derive, re-implement
5. Note the pattern in your journal
6. Star for re-solving in 7 days if hard
```

---

## III. WHY UPSOLVING WORKS
- The problem is fresh in your mind
- You've already invested struggle (primed to learn)
- The gap is precisely identified (you know what you couldn't do)
- Re-implementing cements the technique

---

## IV. THE EDITORIAL DISCIPLINE
- Read for the IDEA, not the code
- Stop reading once you understand the approach
- Re-derive the rest yourself
- Implement WITHOUT copy-paste

---

## V. CATEGORIZING UPSOLVES
For each upsolved problem, identify WHY you missed it:
- **Knowledge gap**: didn't know the technique → study it
- **Recognition gap**: knew it, didn't see it → solve more of this pattern
- **Execution gap**: saw it, coded it wrong → drill implementation
- **Time gap**: would've solved with more time → speed practice

---

## VI. THE UPSOLVE CADENCE
- Upsolve within 24-48 hours of a contest (while fresh)
- Don't let unsolved problems pile up
- A contest without upsolving taught you almost nothing

---

## VII. THE COMPOUNDING EFFECT
Each upsolve adds a pattern to your library. Over 100 contests, that's hundreds of patterns absorbed. This is how Experts become Masters.

---

## VIII. THE STAR-AND-REVISIT SYSTEM
- Star hard upsolves
- Re-solve them in 7 days (test retention)
- Re-solve again in 30 days if still shaky
- Spaced repetition for problems
