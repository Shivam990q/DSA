# 📖 Editorial Reading

> *"Read for the idea, not the code. The 'aha' is the gold."*

---

## I. THE DISCIPLINE
An editorial is a teacher. But read incorrectly, it teaches nothing (you nod along, then can't reproduce it).

---

## II. THE CORRECT METHOD
```
1. Attempt the problem first (30-60 min)
2. Read editorial ONLY until you grasp the key idea
3. STOP reading
4. Close the editorial
5. Re-derive the solution yourself
6. Implement WITHOUT copy-paste
7. Note the pattern
```

---

## III. WHAT TO EXTRACT
- **The key observation** (the "aha")
- **The pattern category**
- **The complexity argument**
- **Any new technique** you learned
- **Why YOU didn't see it** (the gap)

---

## IV. THE DAILY EDITORIAL HABIT
Read 1 editorial per day, even of problems you didn't attempt. This floods your pattern library passively.

After 365 days: 365 editorials → hundreds of patterns absorbed.

---

## V. WHERE TO FIND GREAT EDITORIALS
- **[Codeforces](https://codeforces.com)** official editorials (per round)
- **[AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp)** editorials (excellent quality)
- **[USACO Guide](https://usaco.guide)** (with explanations)
- **[Errichto](https://www.youtube.com/@Errichto) / [William Lin](https://www.youtube.com/@tmwilliamlin168)** (YouTube walkthroughs)
- **[CP-Algorithms.com](https://cp-algorithms.com)** (technique references)

---

## VI. THE ANTI-PATTERN
❌ Read editorial after 5 min of attempting
❌ Copy-paste the code
❌ Nod along without re-deriving
❌ Never re-implement

These produce ZERO learning despite feeling productive.

---

## VII. THE "WHY DIDN'T I SEE IT" QUESTION
The most valuable question after reading any editorial:
> "What observation did I miss, and why?"

The answer reveals your specific blind spot. Fix it by solving more of that pattern.

---

## VIII. EDITORIAL-DRIVEN LEARNING
For rapid growth in a weak topic:
- Read 20 editorials on that topic in a week
- Note each key idea
- Then solve 20 fresh problems applying them

This concentrated exposure breaks plateaus fast.
