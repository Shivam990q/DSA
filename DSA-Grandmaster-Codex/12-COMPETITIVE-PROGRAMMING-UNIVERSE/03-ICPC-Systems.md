# 🏆 ICPC Systems — Team Contest Playbook

> *"ICPC is a team sport. Three minds, one keyboard, five hours of war."*

---

## I. THE FORMAT
- 3 students, 1 computer, 5 hours
- 8-13 problems
- Ranked by problems solved, then total time (with +20 min penalty per wrong submission on solved problems)

---

## II. TEAM ROLES
- **Implementer**: fastest/cleanest coder; types most solutions
- **Algorithmist**: strongest theory; cracks hard problems
- **Captain/Strategist**: tracks scoreboard, manages time, decides what to attempt

Roles rotate based on per-problem strength.

---

## III. THE OPENING (first 30 min)
1. All three grab the problem set; split (~4 each).
2. Quickly classify: easy / medium / hard.
3. Implementer starts the easiest; others read deeper.

---

## IV. THE MIDDLE GAME
- Solve easies fast (bank points + minimize time penalty).
- For mediums: one sketches the approach on paper, hands clean plan to implementer.
- For hards: pair up — algorithmist + implementer.

---

## V. THE COMPUTER DISCIPLINE
With ONE computer:
- THINK on paper; CODE only when the solution is clear.
- Pre-write solutions on paper; transcribe quickly.
- Never "think at the keyboard" — it wastes shared resource.

---

## VI. THE PENALTY MANAGEMENT
- Wrong submission = +20 min (on eventually-solved problems).
- Test locally before submitting.
- Don't spam-submit in panic.

---

## VII. TEAM PRACTICE
- 1 team virtual ICPC per week (5h, on [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com) Gym).
- Upsolve all unsolved over the following week.
- Build chemistry: learn who's best at geometry, DP, graphs, etc.
- Develop shared notation and a team template library ([KACTL](https://github.com/kth-competitive-programming/kactl)-style printout).

---

## VIII. THE PROGRESSION
- Practice contests → local/preliminary rounds → regionals → World Finals
- Top teams solve 8-11 problems; the rest is differentiated by time penalty.

---
