# 📈 Rating Climbing Systems

> *"Rating is a lagging indicator of skill. Build skill; rating follows."*

---

## I. THE TRUTH ABOUT RATING
- Rating reflects skill over MANY contests (variance averages out)
- One contest is noisy; 50 contests reveal your true level
- Don't obsess over single-contest swings

---

## II. THE 3-PHASE CLIMB

### Phase 1: Establish Baseline (1-3 months)
- Solve 30+ problems at current level
- Stabilize your rating
- Identify weakest topics

### Phase 2: Targeted Weaknesses (3-6 months)
- 20+ problems per weak topic
- Theory + practice
- Move to next-tier problems

### Phase 3: Volume (continuous)
- Daily problems above your level
- Weekly contests + upsolving

---

## III. THE DIFFICULTY-TARGETING RULE
Solve problems ~200 rating points ABOVE your current rating. This is the growth edge:
- Too easy → no growth
- Too hard → frustration, no learning
- ~200 above → optimal struggle

---

## IV. THE PLATEAU PROTOCOL
When rating stalls 3+ weeks:
1. Identify the blocking topic (the one you avoid)
2. Topical immersion: 50 problems on it in 1-2 weeks
3. Re-engage rated contests

---

## V. THE METRICS TO TRACK
- Rating over time (trend, not single points)
- Problems solved per week
- Average difficulty solved
- Topics mastered
- Mistake categories eliminated

---

## VI. RATING GOALS BY TIMELINE (realistic, consistent practice)
| Timeline | [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com) target  |
|----------|--------------------|
| 3 months | Pupil → Specialist |
| 6 months | Specialist         |
| 1 year   | Expert             |
| 2 years  | CM                 |
| 3-4 years| Master             |

---

## VII. THE MINDSET
- Compare with yesterday-you, never with Tourist
- Rating drops are variance + learning opportunities
- The climb is non-linear (plateaus then leaps)
- Consistency over intensity

---

## VIII. AVOIDING RATING OBSESSION
- Practice for SKILL, not rating
- Rating is a side effect of devotion to the work
- Pursue rating directly → you'll game it and stall
- Pursue skill → rating becomes inevitable
