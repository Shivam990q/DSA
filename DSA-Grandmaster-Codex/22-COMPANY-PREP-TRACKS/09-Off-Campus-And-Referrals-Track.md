# 🎯 Off-Campus & Referrals Track

> *"On-campus is a lottery you may not be in. Off-campus + referrals is a game you control."*

How to land roles when you don't have campus placement, or want better companies than your campus offers.

---

## THE THREE OFF-CAMPUS PATHS

### 1. Referrals ⭐ (highest yield)
A warm referral can 3-5× your callback rate vs cold applications.

**How to get referrals:**
1. Find employees at target companies (LinkedIn search: "company + role")
2. Build a genuine connection — engage with their content, send a thoughtful message
3. Show your work (GitHub, projects, contest profile, resume)
4. Politely ask: "I'm applying for [role]. Would you be open to referring me? Here's my resume."
5. Make it EASY for them (give them everything they need)

**Don't**: cold-beg, mass-spam, be entitled. Build relationships.

### 2. Direct Applications
- Apply on official career pages (most reliable)
- Set up job alerts
- Apply EARLY (many roles fill fast)
- Tailor your resume to each role

### 3. Job Platforms
- **LinkedIn** ⭐ (alerts + Easy Apply + recruiter outreach)
- **[Wellfound](https://wellfound.com) (AngelList)** — startups
- **[Instahyre](https://www.instahyre.com), Cutshort, Hirist, [Naukri](https://www.naukri.com)** (India)
- **Hired, Triplebyte-style** matching
- **Otta, Welcome to the Jungle** (curated)
- Telegram job-alert channels (verify on official pages)

---

## THE OFF-CAMPUS STRATEGY (numbers game + skill)
```
1. Build a strong profile (resume + GitHub + LeetCode/CF + projects)
2. Make a tiered target list (dream / realistic / safe) — 50+ companies
3. Get referrals where possible (LinkedIn networking)
4. Apply in waves (track everything)
5. Prep continuously (DSA + CS subjects + the company's track)
6. Interview, learn, iterate
7. One offer is enough — but you need many shots
```

## BUILDING THE PROFILE THAT GETS NOTICED
- **Resume**: clean, 1 page, impact-focused (see [`10-Resume-And-Profile-Building.md`](./10-Resume-And-Profile-Building.md))
- **GitHub**: 2-3 real, well-documented projects
- **[LeetCode](https://leetcode.com)**: a solid solved count + contest rating (signals consistency)
- **[Codeforces](https://codeforces.com)**: rating (for CP-heavy companies)
- **LinkedIn**: complete, with projects + activity
- **Portfolio site** (optional but nice)

---

## NETWORKING (the underrated skill)
- Attend meetups, hackathons, conferences
- Engage genuinely on LinkedIn/Twitter (comment, share, build)
- Help others (answer questions, contribute to OSS)
- Alumni networks
- Build relationships BEFORE you need them

---

## HANDLING REJECTION
- Rejection is the default, not the exception (even strong candidates face many)
- It's a numbers + skill game — improve both
- Every rejection = data (what to improve)
- Persistence is the meta-skill

---

## THE TIMELINE REALITY
- Off-campus job hunting can take 2-6 months of active effort
- Apply to 50-100+ companies across tiers
- Keep practicing throughout
- Don't get discouraged by silence (most applications get no response — that's normal)
