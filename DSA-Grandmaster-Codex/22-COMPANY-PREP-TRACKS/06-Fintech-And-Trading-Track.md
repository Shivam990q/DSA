# 💹 Fintech & Quant/Trading Track

> Jane Street, Hudson River Trading, Optiver, IMC, Tower Research, Citadel/Citadel Securities, Two Sigma, DE Shaw, Jump Trading, WorldQuant + fintech (Stripe, Plaid, Coinbase covered in unicorns).

The **highest compensation in tech** for new grads. A DIFFERENT game: math, probability, and low-latency systems matter more than [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com) grinding.

---

## THE HIRING FUNNEL (quant/HFT)
```
1. Resume screen (math/CS pedigree, competitive math/CP background helps a lot)
2. Online test — probability, math, mental math, sometimes coding
3. Phone/onsite rounds:
   - Probability & statistics ⭐
   - Mental math / estimation
   - Brain teasers / market-making games
   - Coding (DSA + sometimes low-latency C++)
   - Sometimes: trading games, "fair value" exercises
4. Offer
```

---

## WHAT THEY TEST (very different from FAANG)
1. **Probability & Statistics** ⭐⭐ — the core (expected value, conditional probability, distributions, Markov chains, martingales, Bayesian)
2. **Mental math** ⭐ — fast arithmetic, estimation (Fermi problems)
3. **Brain teasers / puzzles** — logic, game theory
4. **Combinatorics**
5. **Coding** — DSA (often), and **low-latency C++** for engineering roles
6. **Market intuition** — for trading roles (games like "guess the number," market-making)

---

## ROLE TYPES
- **Quant Trader** — heavy probability, games, market intuition; less coding
- **Quant Researcher** — math, stats, ML, modeling
- **Quant Developer / SWE** — low-latency C++, systems, DSA + the math

---

## THE PREP PLAN (4-6 months, math-heavy)
```
Month 1-2: PROBABILITY & STATISTICS ⭐
  - Expected value, variance, conditional probability, Bayes
  - Distributions, Markov chains, random walks, martingales
Month 3:   BRAIN TEASERS + mental math + combinatorics
Month 4:   DSA (LeetCode) + low-latency C++ (for dev roles)
Month 5-6: Mock interviews + practice trading games + apply
```

## THE RESOURCE STACK ⭐
### Books
- **A Practical Guide to Quantitative Finance Interviews** (Xinfeng Zhou) — "the Green Book" ⭐⭐ (the bible)
- **Heard on the Street** (Timothy Crack) — quant brainteasers ⭐
- **Fifty Challenging Problems in Probability** (Mosteller) ⭐
- **Quant Job Interview Questions and Answers** (Joshi)
- **Probability and Computing** (Mitzenmacher, Upfal)
- **Introduction to Probability** (Blitzstein & Hwang — also free [Harvard Stat 110](https://projects.iq.harvard.edu/stat110)) ⭐

### Courses / practice
- **Harvard Stat 110** (Joe Blitzstein, free on YouTube) ⭐
- **[Project Euler](https://projecteuler.net)** (math + programming)
- Competitive programming background ([Codeforces](https://codeforces.com)) is a strong signal
- **[brainstellar](https://brainstellar.com)** — quant puzzles
- Mental math drills ([Zetamac](https://arithmetic.zetamac.com) arithmetic game)

### For dev roles
- Low-latency C++ (memory, cache, concurrency, no-allocation hot paths) — see [`../01-LEVELS-PROGRESSION/Level-08-Algorithm-Engineering.md`](../01-LEVELS-PROGRESSION/Level-08-Algorithm-Engineering.md)
- LeetCode for DSA

---

## THE BAR
- **Strong probability/math** is non-negotiable
- Fast mental math
- For dev: excellent C++ + systems knowledge
- Competitive math (olympiad) or CP (Codeforces) background is a major plus

---

## THE STRATEGY
- This is the **most math-intensive** track — if you love probability/math, it's the highest-paying path.
- A strong **Codeforces rating** or **math olympiad** background opens doors here.
- Practice **mental math daily** (Zetamac) and **probability puzzles** (Green Book, Heard on the Street).
- Trading roles ≠ dev roles — know which you're targeting.

## FINTECH (non-HFT) — Stripe, Plaid, Coinbase, Razorpay, PayPal
These are more like [unicorns](./03-Unicorns-And-Product-Startups-Track.md)/big tech:
- DSA + system design + practical coding

- Less probability-heavy than HFT
