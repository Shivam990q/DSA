# 📖 Studying Errichto's Editorials

> *"Errichto (Kamil Debowski) is among the best algorithm teachers in the world. His editorials teach not just solutions, but HOW to think."*

---

## I. WHY ERRICHTO
- Two-time IOI gold medalist, top [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com) competitor
- Exceptional teacher: explains the THOUGHT PROCESS, not just the answer
- YouTube channel + Codeforces blogs are gold for self-learners

---

## II. WHAT HIS EDITORIALS TEACH
### 1. The thought process
He shows how to ARRIVE at the solution: "First I tried X, it failed because Y, so I thought about Z."

### 2. Multiple approaches
He often shows brute force → optimization, demonstrating the journey.

### 3. Implementation tips
Clean coding, debugging, stress-testing — practical skills.

### 4. Common mistakes
He highlights where people go wrong.

---

## III. HOW TO STUDY EDITORIALS (his or any)
1. **Attempt the problem first** (30-60 min).
2. **Read until you grasp the IDEA**, then stop.
3. **Close the editorial. Re-derive.**
4. **Implement from understanding** (no copy-paste).
5. **Note the pattern** in your journal.

---

## IV. THE EDITORIAL-READING DISCIPLINE
- Read for the IDEA, not the code.
- The "aha" moment is the gold — internalize it.
- Ask: "What observation did I miss? Why didn't I see it?"
- Add the missing observation to your pattern library.

---

## V. ERRICHTO'S TEACHING RESOURCES

## VI. THE META-LESSON
Editorials are the fastest learning accelerant — IF read correctly. Read for the idea, re-derive, implement. Errichto's editorials model exactly how to think through hard problems.

> "Watching someone solve teaches you nothing. Understanding WHY they solved it that way teaches you everything."
