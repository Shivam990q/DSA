# 📅 Daily Training System

> *"Show up. Every day. Even when nothing inside wants to."*

---

## I. THE DAILY MINIMUM (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

```
☐ 1 problem solved with full reflection (45-90 min)
```

That's the **floor**. No matter what. Sick? Wedding? Holiday? **One problem.**

---

## II. THE OPTIMAL DAILY ROUTINE (3 hours)

### Morning (45 min) — Theory
- Read 1 chapter from a CP book or cp-algorithms.com page
- Implement 1 algorithm from scratch
- Watch 1 short tutorial video (optional)

### Afternoon (90 min) — Practice
- 10 min: warmup with easy problem
- 60 min: target problem (timed, NO editorial first 30 min)
- 20 min: editorial study + re-implement

### Evening (45 min) — Review
- 15 min: update mistake log + pattern journal
- 15 min: re-implement yesterday's problem from scratch (memory test)
- 15 min: plan tomorrow's topic / problem

---

## III. THE DAILY CHECKLIST

```
☐ I attempted at least one problem
☐ I spent ≥30 min before peeking at any solution
☐ I tested edge cases before submitting
☐ I noted the pattern in my journal
☐ I wrote 3 sentences about what I learned
☐ I did not multitask (no phone, no Discord, no music with lyrics)
☐ I planned tomorrow's topic before sleeping
```

7/7 = optimal day. 5/7 = solid day. <3/7 = day to improve.

---

## IV. THE PROBLEM-SOLVING RITUAL (per problem)

```
1. Read problem 3× (skim, detail, adversarial)
2. Restate in your own words
3. Note constraints → infer algorithm class
4. Note keywords → infer pattern category
5. Try small examples by hand
6. Look for invariants, monotonicity, symmetries
7. Write brute force (mentally or actually)
8. Identify bottleneck
9. Design optimization
10. Convince yourself of correctness
11. Code clean
12. Test on examples + edge cases
13. Submit
14. Reflect: 3 sentences in journal
```

---

## V. WHAT TO SOLVE WHEN

### Monday: New topic introduction
1 theory chapter + 3 fresh problems on that topic.

### Tuesday: Topic deepening
5 problems all on Monday's topic. Different difficulty levels.

### Wednesday: Mixed
5-7 problems with random tags. Tests pattern recognition.

### Thursday: Hard problem day
1 problem, 90+ minutes, deep dive. Editorial only after 60 min.

### Friday: Live contest
[Codeforces](https://codeforces.com) Div 2/3 or [AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp) ABC. Full duration.

### Saturday: Upsolve + mock
Upsolve Friday's contest fully. 1 mock interview.

### Sunday: Reflection + light
Review week's mistakes. Re-implement 2-3 problems from memory. Light study.

---

## VI. ENERGY MANAGEMENT

### Sleep
- 7-9 hours, consistent timing
- Naps OK, but don't replace nighttime sleep
- Brain consolidates patterns during sleep

### Food
- Protein every meal
- Avoid heavy carbs before practice (sleepiness)
- Hydrate during long sessions

### Movement
- 30 min/day minimum
- Walk after sessions (blood flow → memory consolidation)
- Strength training 3×/week

### Mental
- 10 min meditation morning (focus training)
- Phone in another room during practice
- 1 day/week fully off (Sabbath protects against burnout)

---

## VII. THE 5-MINUTE WARMUP

Before any practice session, do 5 minutes of "easy fluency":
- Solve a previously-solved easy problem from memory
- Re-implement a known algorithm (BFS, BS, DSU)
- Type a template

This primes the mind. Like an athlete's stretches.

---

## VIII. AVOIDING EXCUSES

| Excuse                           | Counter                                          |
|----------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| "I'm tired."                     | One problem. Easy one. Identity > intensity.     |
| "I have no time."                | 30 min on phone for 1 problem.                   |
| "Today's topic is hard."         | Solve an easier one in same topic. Build up.     |
| "I'm not in the mood."           | Mood follows action, not vice versa. Start.      |
| "I'll do double tomorrow."       | Tomorrow you = today you. No.                    |
| "I'm out of ideas."              | Open [NeetCode](https://neetcode.io), pick random unsolved.             |

---

## IX. THE DAILY METRICS

Track in spreadsheet:

| Date     | Problems solved | Time | Topic | Difficulty | Patterns learned | Bugs fixed | Mistakes |
|----------|----------------|------|-------|------------|------------------|-----------|----------|
| 2026-05-29 | 3 | 2h | DP | 1500 | Knuth opt | Off-by-1 | Mod overflow |

After 90 days, patterns emerge. Strengths/weaknesses. Velocity. Trend lines.

---

## X. THE 365-DAY PROMISE

If you do ONE problem (with reflection) every day for 365 days:
- 365 problems solved
- ~50-100 patterns internalized
- Identity locked in: "I am someone who codes daily"
- Rating: easily 200-500 points up

In CP terms: Newbie → Specialist. Or: Specialist → Expert. Anyone can do this.
