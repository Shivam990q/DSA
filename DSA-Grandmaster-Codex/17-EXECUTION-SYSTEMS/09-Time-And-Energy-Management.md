# ⏱️ Time & Energy Management

> *"You don't manage time. You manage energy. Time just passes."*

---

## I. ENERGY > TIME

Two hours of high-energy deep focus beats eight hours of low-energy distraction.

Manage the four energy reservoirs:
1. **Physical** (sleep, food, exercise)
2. **Mental** (focus, deep work)
3. **Emotional** (motivation, mood)
4. **Spiritual** (purpose, meaning)

---

## II. THE PHYSICAL FOUNDATION

### Sleep — the master variable
- 7-9 hours, consistent schedule
- The brain consolidates patterns during sleep (especially REM)
- Sleep deprivation cuts problem-solving by 30-50%
- **A skipped night = a wasted day of learning**

### Nutrition
- Protein + complex carbs for sustained energy
- Avoid sugar spikes (crash mid-session)
- Hydrate (dehydration → brain fog)
- Caffeine: helps focus, but not after 2pm (disrupts sleep)

### Exercise
- 30 min/day minimum
- Cardio boosts neurogenesis (literally grows brain cells)
- Walking after study consolidates memory
- Strength training 3×/week for overall health

---

## III. THE 90-MINUTE ULTRADIAN RHYTHM

The brain works in ~90-minute cycles of high focus, then needs rest.

```
[90 min DEEP WORK] → [15-20 min REST] → [90 min DEEP WORK] → ...
```

3 cycles/day = ~4.5 hours of monstrous output. More than that → diminishing returns.

---

## IV. DEEP WORK PROTOCOL

To enter deep focus:
1. **Eliminate distractions**: phone in another room, notifications off
2. **Single task**: one problem, full attention
3. **Time-box**: set a 90-min timer
4. **Ritual**: same desk, same prep, signals "focus time"

Distraction is the enemy. One interruption costs ~20 min to refocus.

---

## V. THE POMODORO VARIANT

For shorter sessions:
```
25 min focus → 5 min break (×4) → 30 min long break
```

Good for: building the habit, low-energy days, breaking inertia.

---

## VI. MANAGING THE EMOTIONAL RESERVOIR

### Motivation is unreliable
Don't depend on it. Build systems (habits, environment) that work without motivation.

### Mood follows action
Don't wait to "feel like it." Start, and the feeling follows.

### Celebrate small wins
Solved a hard problem? Acknowledge it. Dopamine sustains the habit.

### Avoid comparison-despair
Tourist solves Div1F in 10 min. He's been at it 15+ years. Compare only with yesterday-you.

---

## VII. THE WEEKLY ENERGY AUDIT

```
This week:
☐ Slept 7+ hours most nights?
☐ Exercised 3+ times?
☐ Ate well (protein, hydration)?
☐ Had 1 full rest day?
☐ Avoided burnout signs?
☐ Maintained deep-work blocks?
```

If energy is low, FIX THE FOUNDATION before pushing harder. A tired brain learns nothing.

---

## VIII. BURNOUT — PREVENTION & RECOVERY

### Warning signs
- Dreading practice
- Zombie-mode sessions (time spent, nothing learned)
- Irritability, sleep issues
- Skipping multiple days

### Prevention
- 1 full rest day/week
- Sustainable volume (2h/day beats 8h/day burnout cycles)
- Variety (don't grind only one painful topic forever)

### Recovery
- 1 full week off (no problems, no theory)
- Fiction, travel, friends, nature
- Return at 50% volume; ramp slowly

**Burnout is reversible. Quitting is not. Rest is part of the work.**

---

## IX. TIME-BLOCKING

Plan your day in blocks:
```
06:00-07:30  Deep work (theory + 1 hard problem)
07:30-09:00  Breakfast, commute
09:00-17:00  Day job / college
19:00-20:30  Deep work (practice)
20:30-21:00  Reflection + plan tomorrow
22:30        Sleep
```

What gets scheduled gets done. Vague intentions evaporate.

---

## X. THE COMPOUND EFFECT

> Skill(t+1) = Skill(t) × (1 + r)

Where r depends on energy. High-energy days have high r. Burnout days have negative r (skill decays).

**Consistent moderate energy beats sporadic high energy.** The river beats the flood.
