# 🗓️ Quarterly & Yearly Planning

> *"Days are tactics. Quarters are strategy. Years are destiny."*

---

## I. THE QUARTERLY CYCLE (90 days)

A quarter is long enough to master a domain, short enough to stay focused.

```
Quarter structure:
  Month 1: Acquire (learn new domain deeply)
  Month 2: Apply (solve heavily in that domain)
  Month 3: Integrate (mix with prior knowledge + contests)
```

## VI. SETTING YEARLY GOALS (SMART)

Goals should be:
- **Specific**: "Reach CF Expert" not "get good"
- **Measurable**: rating number, problem count
- **Achievable**: realistic given hours available
- **Relevant**: aligned with your end goal (job vs CP vs research)
- **Time-bound**: by Dec 31

Example:
> "By December, reach Codeforces Expert (1600+), solve 600 problems, complete the CSES problem set, and place top-50 in 2 contests."

---

## VII. THE ANNUAL REVIEW (December ritual, 2-3 hours)

1. **Re-read** all 4 quarterly reviews.
2. **Plot** your rating/skill trajectory.
3. **Celebrate** wins (be specific).
4. **Diagnose** failures (what blocked progress?).
5. **Set** next year's theme + 3-5 yearly goals.
6. **Re-read** the philosophy docs (Mindset, Grandmaster Code, Geeta).

---

## VIII. ADJUSTING WHEN OFF-TRACK

If a quarter underperforms:
- **Diagnose**: time? consistency? wrong focus? burnout? life events?
- **Adjust the plan, not the identity.** You're still on the path.
- **Reduce scope** if overcommitted. Better to hit a smaller goal than miss a big one.

---

## IX. THE LONG VIEW

> *"In 1 year, you can become interview-ready.  
>  In 3 years, a strong competitor.  
>  In 5 years, an expert.  
>  In 10 years, a master.  
>  The only variable is whether you keep walking."*

Plan in quarters. Review in years. Walk daily.
