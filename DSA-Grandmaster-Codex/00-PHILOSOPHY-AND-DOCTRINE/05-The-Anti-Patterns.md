# ☠️ The Anti-Patterns — What Kills Progress

> *"It is not enough to know the right way. You must know the wrong way well enough to never tread it."*

---

## ⚠️ The 21 Career-Killing Anti-Patterns

### 1. **Tutorial Hell**
*"I'll watch one more video."*
**Symptom**: 200 hours of YouTube; 2 problems solved.
**Cure**: 1 video → 5 problems → next video. Hard rule.

### 2. **The Hopper**
Jumps from CLRS → Sedgewick → CP Handbook → [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com) → [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com) → reset every 2 weeks.
**Symptom**: Many half-read books, no skill.
**Cure**: Pick ONE resource. Finish it. Then judge it.

### 3. **The Memorizer**
Memorizes 200 LeetCode solutions. Cannot solve anything new.
**Symptom**: Crashes on slight variations.
**Cure**: Force yourself to derive every algorithm from scratch. No template-pasting.

### 4. **The Easy Problem Addict**
Solves 1000 easy problems on LeetCode. Rating doesn't grow.
**Symptom**: Comfort-zone bias. Avoidance of struggle.
**Cure**: 60% of problems should be at-or-above your current skill ceiling.

### 5. **The Solution Peeker**
Looks at the editorial after 5 minutes. Every problem.
**Symptom**: Cannot solve anything without help.
**Cure**: Hard rule — 30 minutes of attempt before any peek.

### 6. **The Theory-Only Reader**
Has read CLRS twice. Still cannot implement quicksort.
**Symptom**: Knowledge that doesn't reach fingers.
**Cure**: 1 chapter read = 5 problems implemented.

### 7. **The Practice-Only Coder**
1000 problems solved. Still confused by Master Theorem.
**Symptom**: No theoretical foundation. Stuck at intermediate level forever.
**Cure**: Theory-practice ratio should be 30:70.

### 8. **The Language Tourist**
Switches between C++, Python, Java, Rust, Go, Kotlin every month.
**Symptom**: Mediocre in all. Master of none.
**Cure**: Pick one language. Master it (containers, idioms, debugging). Then add others.

### 9. **The Single-Pattern Hammer**
Solves every problem with DP. Or BFS. Or hash maps.
**Symptom**: Forces wrong approach onto problems.
**Cure**: Daily varied problem types. Never two same-pattern problems back-to-back.

### 10. **The No-Test Coder**
Submits without testing. Gets WA. Gets WA. Gets WA.
**Symptom**: 50% of submissions fail.
**Cure**: Every problem → test on examples + 3 self-generated edge cases before submission.

### 11. **The Magic-Number Coder**
Code full of `1e9+7`, `(1<<20)`, `0x3f3f3f3f` with no comments.
**Symptom**: Future-self cannot read past-self's code.
**Cure**: `const int MOD = 1e9 + 7;` always. Name your constants.

### 12. **The Premature Optimizer**
Spends 2 hours optimizing a non-bottleneck.
**Symptom**: Still hasn't solved the problem.
**Cure**: Brute force first. Optimize bottleneck only. Profile, don't guess.

### 13. **The Overthinker**
Suspects every problem of being a hidden segment tree.
**Symptom**: Misses easy problems. Submits over-engineered solutions.
**Cure**: ALWAYS write the dumb solution first. Always.

### 14. **The Underthinker**
Codes the first idea that pops into head.
**Symptom**: Half-finished solutions, wrong approach.
**Cure**: 5-minute "design phase" before any code. Force pen-and-paper.

### 15. **The Perfectionist**
Won't submit until code is "clean."
**Symptom**: Misses contest deadlines.
**Cure**: Clean during practice. Speed during contest. Refactor after.

### 16. **The Burnout Beast**
8 hours/day for 2 weeks. Then 2 weeks off in despair.
**Symptom**: Inconsistent. Never compounds.
**Cure**: 2 hours/day for 2 years > 8 hours/day for 1 month.

### 17. **The Discord Drifter**
Spends 5 hours discussing problems. Solves 0.
**Symptom**: All input, no output.
**Cure**: Discord/forums max 30 min/day, AFTER your problem-solving session.

### 18. **The Status Hunter**
Solves only problems that look impressive. Skips fundamentals.
**Symptom**: Can solve 2500-rated CF, fails on 1200-rated.
**Cure**: Fill all foundational gaps. Ego is the killer of growth.

### 19. **The Excuse Manufacturer**
"I'm too old." "My CS background is weak." "I don't have a good laptop."
**Symptom**: Reasons multiply, problems unsolved.
**Cure**: Constraints are universal. Excuses are personal. Drop them.

### 20. **The Compare-and-Despair**
Sees Tourist's submission. Despairs. Quits for the day.
**Symptom**: Demotivation. Procrastination.
**Cure**: Compare only with yesterday's you. Tourist's path is not your path.

### 21. **The No-Reflection Practitioner**
Solves problem. Closes tab. Forgets.
**Symptom**: Solves 500 problems, learns from 50.
**Cure**: After every problem: write 3 sentences. *What did I learn? What pattern? What mistake?*

## 🛑 The Three Mortal Sins

These three will kill your trajectory dead:

### Mortal Sin 1: **Inconsistency**
0 problems for 2 weeks; 30 problems in one weekend; 0 again.
> *Skill is not built in pulses. Skill is built in rivers.*

### Mortal Sin 2: **Pretending to Understand**
Nodding while reading editorial; not actually understanding.
> *The brain is not fooled. Pretend understanding produces real ignorance.*

### Mortal Sin 3: **Confusing Effort with Progress**
"I studied for 5 hours today!" — but 4 hours scrolling Twitter, 1 hour distracted reading.
> *Time-on-task ≠ time-on-target. Measure deep work, not screen time.*

---

## 🩹 The Recovery Protocol

If you find yourself in any anti-pattern:

1. **Acknowledge** without shame. Anti-patterns are universal.
2. **Diagnose**: which anti-pattern, which trigger?
3. **Replace**: substitute the wrong behavior with a right one.
4. **Track**: log it daily for 2 weeks.
5. **Forgive**: relapse is part of the path.

---

## 🔮 The Self-Audit (Weekly Sunday Ritual)

Every Sunday, 15 minutes:

- [ ] Did I avoid Tutorial Hell this week?
- [ ] Did I solve at-or-above-my-level problems?
- [ ] Did I stress-test all my solutions?
- [ ] Did I read editorials of unsolved problems?
- [ ] Did I reflect after each problem?
- [ ] Did I sleep, eat, exercise enough?
- [ ] Did I compare myself only with yesterday-me?

Score yourself. Adjust. Continue.
