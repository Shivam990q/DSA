# 🧘 The Discipline System — Daily Practice Doctrine

> *"Motivation gets you started. Discipline keeps you going."*

---

## I. THE CORE TENET

**Discipline is not punishment. Discipline is freedom.**

The disciplined coder doesn't decide every morning "should I practice today?" — that's exhausting and vulnerable to mood. The disciplined coder *practices* the way humans *breathe* — automatic, identity-level, non-negotiable.

---

## II. THE DAILY MINIMUM (Non-Negotiable)

If you do **only one thing**, do this:

```
☐ 1 problem solved with full reflection (45-90 min)
```

That's it. **Every day. No exceptions.** Holiday? Sick? Wedding? **One problem.** It can be easy on bad days. But it must be solved + reflected upon.

This is your **minimum viable discipline**. It builds the identity: *"I am someone who codes daily."*

---


---

## V. THE MONTHLY CYCLE

### Week 1: **Foundation Reinforcement**
Pick a foundational topic you're shaky on. Drill 30 problems.

### Week 2: **Frontier Expansion**
Pick a new topic at the edge of your ability. Theory + 20 problems.

### Week 3: **Contest Heavy**
4-5 live contests. Aggressive upsolving.

### Week 4: **Reflection & Pattern Crystallization**
Review month's mistakes. Re-implement key algorithms from scratch. Write a blog/note summarizing the month's lessons.

---

## VI. THE QUARTERLY MILESTONE

Every 90 days, ask:

1. **Knowledge added**: Which topics did I master this quarter?
2. **Rating delta**: CF / AtCoder / LC rating change?
3. **Mistake categories**: Which bugs am I no longer making?
4. **Weakness identified**: What's my next 90-day focus?
5. **Identity shift**: Do I now think like a stronger version of myself?

## VIII. ENERGY MANAGEMENT

Discipline without energy is despair. Energy is the prerequisite.

### Sleep
- 7-9 hours, consistently. Non-negotiable.
- Brain consolidates patterns during sleep. **Skipped sleep = skipped learning.**

### Nutrition
- Protein every meal.
- Avoid sugar crashes during practice.
- Hydration before / during long sessions.

### Movement
- 30 min/day minimum. Walk, gym, anything.
- Sitting too long = brain fog. Pomodoro + walk.

### Mental
- Meditation 10 min/day (focuses attention).
- 1 day/week fully off (no problems, no theory). Sabbath protects against burnout.

### Social
- 1 person you discuss problems with — accelerates learning 5x.
- Online community (CP Discord, CF blogs) for daily exposure.

## IX. THE ENVIRONMENT DESIGN

Make discipline easy by designing your environment:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ DESK SETUP                                           │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ✓ Same desk, same time, every day                    │
│ ✓ Editor + browser + templates always pre-opened     │
│ ✓ Pen + paper always within reach                    │
│ ✓ Whiteboard or notebook for derivations             │
│ ✓ Phone in another room                              │
│ ✓ Notifications disabled on laptop                   │
│ ✓ Water bottle, snack, comfortable chair             │
│ ✓ Pomodoro timer set up                              │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Friction kills discipline.** Remove every micro-friction between intent and action.

## X. HABIT STACKING

Stack DSA on existing habits:
- Right after morning coffee → solve 1 problem
- During commute (audio) → listen to algo podcast
- After dinner → 1 problem
- Before sleep → 5 min review of today's solution

The stack becomes automatic. The automatic becomes effortless. The effortless compounds.

---

## XI. DEALING WITH SETBACKS

### Setback 1: **Missed a day**
Solution: Don't let it become two days. Get back tomorrow. Don't try to "make up" — that's punishment, not discipline.

### Setback 2: **Bad contest performance**
Solution: 24 hours of zero practice. Then upsolve the contest mercilessly. Performance is feedback, not identity.

### Setback 3: **Plateau (3 weeks no rating gain)**
Solution: Topical immersion week. Pick the topic you avoid. Drown in it.

### Setback 4: **Burnout**
Solution: 1 full week off. Read fiction. Travel. Then return with **reduced volume** but **same consistency**.

### Setback 5: **Life events (loss, illness, major change)**
Solution: Drop to minimum (1 problem/day). Maintain identity. Resume volume when ready.

---

## XII. THE 90-MINUTE LAW

The brain operates in ~90-minute ultradian cycles. Practice in 90-minute deep blocks; rest 15-30 minutes between.

```
[90 min DEEP WORK] → [20 min REST] → [90 min DEEP WORK] → [done]
```

**Three 90-minute blocks/day = 4.5 hours of monstrous output.** More than that and quality drops.

---

## XIII. THE RITUAL OF BEGINNING

Before each practice session, perform a 60-second ritual:

1. Sit. Three deep breaths.
2. Recite: *"I am here to grow. I am here to struggle. I am here to learn."*
3. Open the problem. Read once, slowly.
4. Begin.

This trivial ritual primes the mind. Skip it, and your mind drifts. Honor it, and your mind locks in.

---

## XIV. THE RITUAL OF ENDING

After each problem (solved or not):

1. Write 3 sentences in your reflection log:
   - **What did I learn?**
   - **What pattern did I see (or miss)?**
   - **What would I do differently next time?**

2. Star problems you must revisit in 7 days.

3. Close the editor. Stand. Walk for 2 minutes.

This ends the session cleanly. Your mind closes the loop. Memory consolidates.

---

## XV. THE FINAL DISCIPLINE

> *"Discipline equals freedom. The disciplined are the only ones who are truly free,  
>  for they alone are not slaves to their moods, distractions, and excuses."*  
> — Jocko Willink, paraphrased

When in doubt: **show up.** Show up tired. Show up unmotivated. Show up confused. Just show up.

The showing up *is* the discipline. The discipline *is* the path. The path *is* the mastery.
