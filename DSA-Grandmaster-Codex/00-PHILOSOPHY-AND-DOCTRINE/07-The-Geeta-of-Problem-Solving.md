# 📿 The Geeta of Problem Solving

> *"As the body discards old garments and dons new ones,  
>  so the mind discards naive solutions and dons elegant ones."*  
> *— Adapted from the Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 2, Verse 22*

---

## अध्याय १ — The Chapter of Despair (Arjuna Vishada Yoga)

**Verse 1.** When Arjuna sat before the battlefield of [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com) and saw problems labeled 2400+, his arms trembled, his fingers froze, his mind scattered.

**Verse 2.** "O Krishna," he said, "I cannot fight. These problems are gods. I am but a mortal coder."

**Krishna replied:** "Why this faintness in the hour of struggle? It is unbecoming of one who walks the path. Rise. The problem is not your enemy — your fear is."

---

## अध्याय २ — The Chapter of Karma (Karma Yoga)

> **Verse 2.47**  
> *"You have a right to your effort, never to its results.  
>  Let not the fruits of action be your motive,  
>  nor let your attachment be to inaction."*

**Translation for the coder:**
- You have a right to **practice**, not to a Google offer.
- You have a right to **enter contests**, not to win them.
- Practice not for rating, but for the deepening of skill.
- Let outcomes be the side effect of devotion, not the goal.

---

## अध्याय ३ — The Chapter of Knowledge (Jnana Yoga)

> **Verse 4.38**  
> *"There is no purifier in this world equal to knowledge.  
>  One who is perfected in yoga, in time, finds it within."*

**Knowledge** here means *understanding*, not memorization. Memorized solutions purify nothing. Derivation, proof, intuition — these purify the mind and prepare it for any problem.

---

## अध्याय ४ — The Chapter of Renunciation (Sannyasa Yoga)

> **Verse 5.10**  
> *"He who acts without attachment, surrendering results to the Divine,  
>  is untouched by sin, as a lotus leaf by water."*

**Translation:**
Submit code. Get verdict. **Do not let WA define you. Do not let AC inflate you.**  
The grandmaster is unmoved by both. Each verdict is information; not identity.

---

## अध्याय ५ — The Chapter of Meditation (Dhyana Yoga)

> **Verse 6.5**  
> *"One must elevate oneself by one's own mind, not degrade oneself.  
>  The mind is the friend of the conditioned soul, and also its enemy."*

**Translation:**
Your mind will whisper:
- *"You're not smart enough."*
- *"This is too hard."*
- *"Other people are better than you."*

Your mind is also the only weapon you have. **You must train it to be your friend, not your saboteur.** Sit in stillness before each problem. Let the mind settle. Then strike.

---

## अध्याय ६ — The Chapter of Devotion (Bhakti Yoga)

> **Verse 12.13-14**  
> *"He who hates no creature, who is friendly and compassionate,  
>  free from attachment and ego, equipoised in pleasure and pain,  
>  forgiving — such a one is dear to me."*

**Translation:**
- Hate no problem. Each problem is a teacher.
- Be compassionate to your past-self who couldn't solve this.
- Be unattached to ratings; equipoised in green AC and red WA.
- Forgive your bugs. Then fix them.

---

## अध्याय ७ — The Chapter of Discipline (Niyama Yoga)

> **Verse 18.36-37**  
> *"That which appears like poison at first, but becomes like nectar in the end —  
>  born of clarity and discipline of the mind — is sattvic happiness."*

**Translation:**
- Practice is poison (boring, painful, frustrating) — and becomes nectar (skill, recognition, ease).
- Tutorial-watching is nectar (engaging, fun) — and becomes poison (no skill).
- **Choose the poison that becomes nectar.**

---

## अध्याय ८ — The Chapter of the Eternal (Aksara Brahma Yoga)

> **Verse 8.7**  
> *"Therefore, at all times, remember Me and fight.  
>  With mind and intellect surrendered unto Me,  
>  you shall come to Me without doubt."*

**Translation:**
At all times, remember **the algorithmic mind**. While brushing teeth, while commuting, while in meetings — **think problems**. The mind that lives in the algorithmic universe becomes one with it.
