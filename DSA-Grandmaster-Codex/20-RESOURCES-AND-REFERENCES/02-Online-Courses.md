# 🎓 Online Courses

> *"Free, world-class education. The only barrier is discipline."*

---

## FOUNDATIONAL (free)
- **Harvard [CS50](https://cs50.harvard.edu)** (edX) — best intro to CS
- **MIT 6.0001** (OCW) — intro to Python
- **[freeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org)** (YouTube) — multi-language

## ALGORITHMS (free)
- **[MIT 6.006](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-006-introduction-to-algorithms-spring-2020/) — Introduction to Algorithms** (OCW) ⭐
- **MIT 6.046J — Design and Analysis of Algorithms** (OCW)
- **Princeton Algorithms** (Coursera, Sedgewick) ⭐
- **Stanford CS161** (Tim Roughgarden's "Algorithms Illuminated")
- **Stanford Algorithms Specialization** (Coursera)

## ADVANCED (free)
- **[MIT 6.851](https://courses.csail.mit.edu/6.851/) — Advanced Data Structures** (Erik Demaine) ⭐⭐⭐
- **MIT 6.852 — Distributed Algorithms**
- **[MIT 6.172](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-172-performance-engineering-of-software-systems-fall-2018/) — Performance Engineering** ⭐
- **CMU 15-451 — Algorithm Design and Analysis**
- **Berkeley CS170 — Efficient Algorithms**

## COMPETITIVE PROGRAMMING (free)
- **codeforces.com/edu** ⭐ — segment trees, DP, binary search, suffix structures
- **[USACO Guide](https://usaco.guide)** ⭐ — structured curriculum
- **[AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp) [Educational DP Contest](https://atcoder.jp/contests/dp)**
- **[CSES Problem Set](https://cses.fi/problemset/)** (with the CP Handbook)

## INTERVIEW PREP
- **[NeetCode](https://neetcode.io)** (neetcode.io) — free + paid courses ⭐
- **[AlgoExpert](https://www.algoexpert.io)** (paid)
- **Educative.io** — Grokking series
- **[Tech Interview Handbook](https://www.techinterviewhandbook.org)** (free)

## SYSTEM DESIGN
- **[ByteByteGo](https://bytebytego.com)** (Alex Xu)
- **Grokking the System Design Interview**
- **MIT 6.824 — Distributed Systems** (for depth)

## THEORY / RESEARCH
- **MIT 6.840 — Theory of Computation**
- **Stanford CS254 — Computational Complexity**
- **Various lecture series on YouTube** (Demaine, Roughgarden)

---

## HOW TO USE COURSES
- Pick ONE course at your level. Finish it.
- 1 lecture → solve related problems → don't just watch
- Pair theory courses with practice platforms
