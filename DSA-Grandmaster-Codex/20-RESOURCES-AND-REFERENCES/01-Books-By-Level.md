# 📖 Books By Level

> *"The right book at the wrong level wastes both. Match the book to your stage."*

---

## LEVEL 0 (Programming Foundations)
- **Think Like a Programmer** — V. Anton Spraul
- **Automate the Boring Stuff with Python** — Al Sweigart (free)
- **Programming: Principles and Practice Using C++** — Bjarne Stroustrup
- **Head First Java/Python** — for visual learners

## LEVEL 1 (Beginner DSA)
- **Grokking Algorithms** — Aditya Bhargava (visual, gentle) ⭐
- **Cracking the Coding Interview** (Ch 1-3) — Gayle McDowell
- **A Common-Sense Guide to Data Structures and Algorithms** — Jay Wengrow

## LEVEL 2 (Academic DSA)
- **Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)** — Ch 1-13 ⭐
- **Algorithms** — Sedgewick & Wayne
- **Algorithm Design** — Kleinberg & Tardos
- **Algorithms** — Jeff Erickson (free, brilliant proofs)

## LEVEL 3-4 (Placement / Product-Based)
- **Cracking the Coding Interview** (full) — McDowell ⭐
- **Elements of Programming Interviews** — Aziz, Lee, Prakash
- **[Competitive Programmer's Handbook](https://cses.fi/book/book.pdf)** — Laaksonen (free)
- **The Algorithm Design Manual** — Skiena

## LEVEL 5 (FAANG)
- **System Design Interview Vol 1 & 2** — Alex Xu
- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** — Kleppmann
- **CTCI** + company-specific prep

## LEVEL 6-7 (Competitive Programming)
- **[Competitive Programming 4](https://cpbook.net)** — Halim & Halim ⭐ (the CP bible)
- **Guide to Competitive Programming** — Laaksonen
- **Concrete Mathematics** — Knuth, Patashnik, Graham (math foundation)
- **Looking for a Challenge** — Polish CP team

## LEVEL 8 (Algorithm Engineering)
- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** — Kleppmann ⭐
- **Database Internals** — Petrov
- **The Art of Multiprocessor Programming** — Herlihy, Shavit
- **Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective** — Bryant, O'Hallaron
- **Hacker's Delight** — Warren

## LEVEL 9-10 (Research / Inventor)
- **Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach** — Arora, Barak
- **The Art of Computer Programming (TAOCP)** — Knuth
- **Approximation Algorithms** — Vazirani
- **The Design of Approximation Algorithms** — Williamson, Shmoys
- **Randomized Algorithms** — Motwani, Raghavan
- **Computational Geometry** — de Berg et al.
- **Algorithms on Strings, Trees, and Sequences** — Gusfield

---

## THE SELECTIVITY PRINCIPLE
Don't buy 20 books. At each level, pick 1-2 and FINISH them. A finished book beats 10 half-read ones.
