# 📋 Curated Practice Sheets — The Complete Catalog

> *"A sheet is a map. Pick one, follow it to the end, then judge it. Sheet-hopping is a trap."*

These are the battle-tested problem lists used by millions to crack interviews and placements. Each is a structured path so you never wonder "what next?"

---

## 🏆 THE TOP-TIER SHEETS (pick ONE primary)

### 1. Striver's A2Z DSA Course/Sheet ⭐ (most comprehensive, FREE)
- **What**: ~450+ problems, basics → advanced (arrays, recursion, trees, graphs, DP, and more)
- **Best for**: complete beginners who want one path from zero to interview-ready
- **Format**: written explanations + video + practice links
- **Where**: [takeuforward.org](https://takeuforward.org)
- **GitHub mirrors**: search "Strivers-A2Z-DSA-Sheet" (community solution repos exist)

### 2. NeetCode 150 / NeetCode 250 ⭐ (most efficient, FREE)
- **What**: 150 (or extended 250) problems organized by the core patterns
- **Best for**: those who learn by watching; efficient interview prep
- **Format**: video walkthroughs + clean code + the "roadmap" graph
- **Where**: [neetcode.io](https://neetcode.io)
- Includes the **NeetCode Roadmap** — a visual dependency graph of topics

### 3. Blind 75 ⭐ (the original FAANG list)
- **What**: 75 most-frequently-asked problems, curated by a Meta engineer (originally on TeamBlind)
- **Best for**: time-crunched interview prep; the "minimum viable" list
- **Format**: just the list; pair with NeetCode/Striver videos for solutions
- **Where**: many free mirrors; NeetCode and [takeuforward](https://takeuforward.org) both cover it

### 4. Striver's SDE Sheet (180 problems) ⭐ (Indian placements favorite)
- **What**: ~180 problems across 30 days, interview-grade
- **Best for**: Indian product company / service company placements
- **Format**: day-wise schedule + videos
- **Where**: [takeuforward.org](https://takeuforward.org)

## ⚠️ THE GOLDEN RULES OF SHEETS
1. **Pick ONE primary sheet.** Finish it. Sheet-hopping = no progress.
2. **A sheet is a curriculum, not a checklist.** Reflect on each problem.
3. **Don't copy solutions.** Attempt → struggle → editorial idea → re-derive.
4. **Track in a spreadsheet** (problem, date, time, pattern, mistakes, revisit date).
5. **Revisit hard problems** at 7 and 30 days (spaced repetition).
