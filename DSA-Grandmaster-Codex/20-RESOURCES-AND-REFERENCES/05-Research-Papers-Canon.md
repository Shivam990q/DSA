# 🔬 Research Papers Canon

> *"Standing on the shoulders of giants means reading what the giants wrote."*

---

## FOUNDATIONAL PAPERS (lifetime list)
1. **Dijkstra (1959)** — "A Note on Two Problems in Connexion with Graphs" (shortest paths)
2. **Cooley & Tukey (1965)** — FFT
3. **Edmonds (1965)** — "Paths, Trees, and Flowers" (matching, polynomial-time)
4. **Hopcroft & Tarjan (1973)** — planarity, DFS-based algorithms
5. **Knuth, Morris, Pratt (1977)** — KMP string matching
6. **Aho & Corasick (1975)** — multi-pattern matching
7. **Tarjan (1972)** — "Depth-First Search and Linear Graph Algorithms" (SCC, bridges)
8. **Karp (1972)** — "Reducibility Among Combinatorial Problems" (NP-completeness)
9. **Cook (1971)** — "The Complexity of Theorem-Proving Procedures" (Cook-Levin)
10. **Kruskal (1956), Prim (1957)** — MST

## ALGORITHM PAPERS
- **Karatsuba (1962)** — fast multiplication
- **Strassen (1969)** — fast matrix multiplication
- **Bellman (1958), Ford** — shortest paths with negative weights
- **Floyd (1962), Warshall** — all-pairs shortest paths
- **Hoare (1962)** — Quicksort
- **Williams (1964)** — Heapsort
- **Fredman & Tarjan (1987)** — Fibonacci heaps

## COMPLEXITY & CRYPTO
- **Rivest, Shamir, Adleman (1978)** — RSA
- **Agrawal, Kayal, Saxena (2002)** — AKS primality (PRIMES in P)
- **Shor (1994)** — quantum factoring
- **Babai (2016)** — graph isomorphism quasipolynomial

## MODERN BREAKTHROUGHS
- **Chen et al. (2022)** — almost-linear max flow
- **Bernstein, Nanongkai, Wulff-Nilsen (2022)** — negative-weight shortest paths near-linear
- Recent matrix-multiplication improvements (Williams, Alman, Duan, Le Gall)

## DISTRIBUTED / SYSTEMS
- **Lamport (1978)** — "Time, Clocks, and the Ordering of Events"
- **Lamport (1998)** — "The Part-Time Parliament" (Paxos)
- **Ongaro & Ousterhout (2014)** — Raft
- **Dean & Ghemawat (2004)** — MapReduce
- **DeCandia et al. (2007)** — Dynamo

---

## WHERE TO FIND THEM
- **arXiv.org** (cs.DS, cs.DM, cs.CC) — free preprints
- **[DBLP](https://dblp.org)** — author/venue search
- **Google Scholar** — citation tracking
- **ECCC** — complexity theory
- University course pages (often link PDFs)

## TOP VENUES
- **SODA** (ACM-SIAM Symposium on Discrete Algorithms)
- **STOC** (Symposium on Theory of Computing)
- **FOCS** (Foundations of Computer Science)
- **ICALP**, **ESA**, **SoCG**
