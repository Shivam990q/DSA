# 📱 Telegram Channels — A Safe Guide

> ⚠️ **Read the safety section first.** Telegram is useful for job alerts and community, but it's also full of scams and pirated content.

---

## ⚠️ SAFETY FIRST (read this)
- **Avoid "premium course" channels** that share PAID courses for free — this is piracy (illegal) and often laced with malware/phishing.
- **Never pay** a random Telegram channel for "guaranteed placement" or "leaked OA questions."
- **Beware** channels asking you to download `.apk`/`.exe` files or click shortened links.
- **Don't share** personal data (resume with phone/address) in public groups.
- Legitimate uses: **job alerts**, **community discussion**, **contest reminders**, **official channels** of creators.

---

## ✅ LEGITIMATE USES OF TELEGRAM FOR PREP

### 1. Off-campus job alerts
Many channels aggregate daily fresher/experienced openings (campus drives, off-campus, internships). Useful for finding opportunities you'd otherwise miss.

**How to find current ones (they change often):**
- Search Telegram for: "off campus jobs", "fresher jobs", "hiring alerts", "software jobs India/your-region"
- Cross-check any posted opening on the company's official career page before applying
- Follow 2-3 reliable ones; mute the rest

### 2. Contest reminders
- [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com) / [AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp) / [CodeChef](https://www.codechef.com) round announcements
- Many community bots post contest schedules

### 3. Creator official channels
- Some YouTubers/educators run official Telegram channels for updates (verify they're official via their YouTube/website link)

### 4. Study communities
- Peer groups for doubt-solving and accountability (find via your college/online community)

---

## 🔍 HOW TO VET A CHANNEL
```
✅ Linked from an official website/YouTube? → likely safe
✅ Only posts links to legitimate free/official resources? → safe
✅ Job alerts that point to official career pages? → useful
❌ Sells "premium courses" cheap? → piracy, avoid
❌ Asks for payment for "guaranteed jobs"? → scam
❌ Shares cracked/leaked content? → illegal + risky
❌ Pushes app downloads or shortened links? → malware risk
```

---

## 🆚 BETTER ALTERNATIVES TO "PREMIUM" TELEGRAM CHANNELS
Everything those piracy channels offer, you can get LEGALLY and FREE:
- DSA: [NeetCode](https://neetcode.io), Striver ([takeuforward](https://takeuforward.org)), [CSES](https://cses.fi/problemset/), [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com) free
- System design: [ByteByteGo](https://bytebytego.com) GitHub, [System Design Primer](https://github.com/donnemartin/system-design-primer) (GitHub)
- CS subjects: [Gate Smashers](https://www.youtube.com/@GateSmashers) (YouTube), [GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org)
- CP: [CP-Algorithms](https://cp-algorithms.com), Codeforces /edu, [USACO Guide](https://usaco.guide)

**You do not need pirated paid courses. The best free resources rival or beat them.**

---

## THE PRINCIPLE
Use Telegram for **job alerts and community**, not as your primary learning source. Keep it to ~15 min/day. Apply to jobs actively; don't doomscroll.
