# 🔔 Job Alert Channels — Finding Opportunities

> *"The best preparation is wasted if you never apply. Hunt opportunities actively."*

---

## WHERE TO FIND OPENINGS

### 1. LinkedIn ⭐
- Set **job alerts** for your role + location
- Follow recruiters at target companies
- Use "Easy Apply" + direct career-page applications
- Post your projects/learning (visibility attracts recruiters)
- Network: connect with employees, ask for referrals (referrals dramatically boost callback rates)

### 2. Company Career Pages (most reliable)
- Apply DIRECTLY on the official site
- Set up alerts where available
- Maintain a target-company list (tiered: dream / realistic / safe)

### 3. Job Boards
- **[Wellfound](https://wellfound.com) (AngelList)** — startups
- **[Instahyre](https://www.instahyre.com), Cutshort, Hirist** (India)
- **[Naukri](https://www.naukri.com)** (India), **Indeed**, **[Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com)**
- **Hired**, **Triplebyte**-style matching platforms
- **Otta / Welcome to the Jungle** (curated tech jobs)

### 4. Telegram Job-Alert Channels
- Aggregate daily off-campus drives + internships
- ⚠️ **Always verify** the posting on the official career page before applying
- Search Telegram for current channels (they change); pick 2-3 reliable ones
- See [`02-Telegram-Channels-Guide.md`](./02-Telegram-Channels-Guide.md) for safety

### 5. Twitter/X
- Many recruiters and startups post openings
- Follow #hiring, company hiring managers

### 6. University / College
- Placement cell (on-campus)
- Alumni network (referrals)

### 7. Open-Source / Community
- Contributing to OSS → visibility → opportunities
- Hackathons → networking → offers

---

## THE REFERRAL STRATEGY ⭐
Referrals are the highest-yield application path:
1. Identify employees at target companies (LinkedIn)
2. Build a genuine connection (don't cold-beg)
3. Show your work (GitHub, projects, contest profile)
4. Politely ask for a referral with your resume + the specific role
5. A warm referral can 3-5× your interview callback rate

- Apply to MANY (rejection is normal; it's a numbers + skill game)
- Tier your targets (dream / realistic / safe)
- Keep practicing DURING interview loops
- One offer is enough — but you need many shots to get it
