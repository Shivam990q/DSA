## 📖 BOOKS — THE CANON

### Tier 1: Foundational (must own / read)
| Book                                                  | Author                      | Audience           | Notes                          |
|-------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)**                 | Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein | All levels        | The Bible. Pseudocode-rich.    |
| **Algorithm Design**                                  | Kleinberg, Tardos           | Undergraduate     | Clearer than CLRS for beginners. |
| **Algorithms**                                        | Sedgewick & Wayne           | Undergraduate     | Java-based, beautifully written. |
| **The Algorithm Design Manual**                       | Steven Skiena               | Practical engineer | War stories from real systems. |
| **Algorithms** (free PDF)                             | Jeff Erickson               | All levels        | Free, brilliant proofs. ⭐       |
| **Concrete Mathematics**                              | Knuth, Patashnik, Graham    | Math foundation   | Lifelong reference.            |

### Tier 2: Deepening
| Book                                                  | Author                      | Use Case                        |
|-------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **[Competitive Programming 4](https://cpbook.net)** (CP4)                   | Halim, Halim                | The CP bible                     |
| **[Competitive Programmer's Handbook](https://cses.fi/book/book.pdf)** (FREE)          | Antti Laaksonen             | CP introduction                  |
| **Guide to Competitive Programming**                  | Laaksonen (Springer)        | Extended CP4 alt                 |
| **Cracking the Coding Interview**                     | Gayle Laakmann McDowell     | Interview prep                   |
| **Elements of Programming Interviews**                | Aziz, Lee, Prakash          | Interview prep (deeper)          |
| **System Design Interview** Vol 1, 2                  | Alex Xu                     | System design prep               |
| **Designing Data-Intensive Applications**             | Martin Kleppmann            | Distributed systems intuition    |

### Tier 3: Advanced & Research
| Book                                                  | Author                      | Use Case                        |
|-------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **The Art of Computer Programming** (TAOCP)           | Donald Knuth                | Lifetime study                   |
| **Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach**       | Arora, Barak                | Complexity theory                |
| **Approximation Algorithms**                          | Vazirani                    | Approximation                    |
| **The Design of Approximation Algorithms**            | Williamson, Shmoys          | Modern approximation             |
| **Randomized Algorithms**                             | Motwani, Raghavan           | Randomization                    |
| **Probability and Computing**                         | Mitzenmacher, Upfal         | Probabilistic methods            |
| **Computational Geometry: Algorithms and Applications** | de Berg et al.            | Geometry                         |
| **Algorithms on Strings, Trees, and Sequences**       | Gusfield                    | Strings deep                     |
| **The Art of Multiprocessor Programming**             | Herlihy, Shavit             | Concurrent DS                    |
| **Convex Optimization**                               | Boyd, Vandenberghe (FREE)   | Optimization                     |
| **Database Internals**                                | Petrov                      | Storage / DB algorithms          |
| **Hacker's Delight**                                  | Henry Warren                | Bit-level tricks                 |
| **Engineering a Compiler**                            | Cooper, Torczon             | Compiler & low-level             |

### Tier 4: Specialized
- **Mining of Massive Datasets** (Leskovec et al.) — free; streaming & ML-related
- **Pearls of Functional Algorithm Design** (Bird) — functional perspective
- **Programming Pearls** (Bentley) — short, beautiful problem narratives
- **Polya, How to Solve It** — problem solving method ⭐

## 🔬 RESEARCH PAPERS (MUST-READ)

### Foundational papers (lifetime list)
1. **Cooley & Tukey (1965)** — FFT
2. **Edmonds (1965)** — "Paths, Trees, and Flowers" (matching)
3. **Hopcroft & Tarjan (1973)** — DFS-based bridges, planarity
4. **Knuth, Morris, Pratt (1977)** — KMP
5. **Aho & Corasick (1975)** — multi-pattern matching
6. **Dijkstra (1959)** — shortest paths
7. **Karp (1972)** — NP-completeness
8. **Cook (1971)** — Cook-Levin theorem (NP-completeness)
9. **Kruskal (1956)**, **Prim (1957)** — MST
10. **Tarjan (1972)** — SCCs

### Modern algorithm papers (selected)
- **Karatsuba (1962)** — fast multiplication
- **Strassen (1969)** — fast matrix multiplication
- **Adleman, Pomerance, Rumely (1983)** — primality
- **Agrawal, Kayal, Saxena (2002)** — AKS primality (deterministic poly time)
- **Rivest, Shamir, Adleman (1978)** — RSA
- **Babai (2016)** — graph isomorphism quasipoly
- **Chen et al. (2022)** — almost-linear max flow

### Where to find them
- **arXiv.org/list/cs.DS** ⭐ — daily preprints
- **[DBLP](https://dblp.org)** — author and venue search
- **ECCC** — Electronic Colloquium on Computational Complexity
- **Google Scholar** — comprehensive

## 🪔 CLOSING NOTE

You don't need ALL of these. Pick:
- **1-2 books** at your level
- **1-2 platforms** for practice
- **1-2 channels** for content
- **1-2 communities** for accountability

Master those. Add more only when you've exhausted them.

The library is infinite. Your time is finite. **Be selective. Go deep, not wide.**
