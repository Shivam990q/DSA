# 🤝 Behavioral Integration — STAR & Company Values

> *"They've confirmed you can code. Now: will you thrive here for years?"*

---

## I. THE STAR METHOD
- **S**ituation: brief context
- **T**ask: your responsibility
- **A**ction: what YOU did (specific, "I" not "we")
- **R**esult: outcome, quantified if possible

Each story: 2-3 minutes.

---

## II. THE 10 CORE STORIES
1. Most challenging technical problem
2. Conflict with a teammate
3. Project failure / lesson
4. Project that exceeded expectations
5. Took initiative unprompted
6. Disagreed with manager
7. Tight deadline + ambiguity
8. Mentored someone
9. Technical decision with tradeoffs
10. Went above and beyond

---

## III. COMPANY VALUE MAPPING

### Amazon — 16 Leadership Principles
Prepare 2-3 stories per LP. Key ones: Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action, Dive Deep, Deliver Results, Have Backbone (Disagree and Commit).

### Google — "Googliness"
Collaboration, comfort with ambiguity, bias to action, continuous learning.

### Meta
Move fast, be bold, focus on impact, build social value.

### Microsoft / Apple
Growth mindset, customer focus, collaboration.

---

## IV. THE PREPARATION DRILL
- Write each story fully (STAR)
- Rehearse aloud, time it (2-3 min)
- Record + review (rambling? vague? "we" overuse?)
- Map each story to 2-3 company values

---

## V. COMMON MISTAKES
- Vague/generic answers
- "We" instead of "I" (own your contribution)
- No measurable result
- Rambling (>4 min)
- Badmouthing past employers
- No reflection/growth shown

---

## VI. THE FOLLOW-UP READINESS
Interviewers probe: "Why did you do that?" "What would you do differently?" "What was the impact?"

Prepare for depth on each story — they'll dig.

---

## VII. QUESTIONS TO ASK THEM
Always have 2-3 thoughtful questions:
- "What does success look like in this role in the first 6 months?"
- "What's the biggest technical challenge the team faces?"
- "How does the team handle technical disagreements?"
