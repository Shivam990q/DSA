# 🎯 LeetCode Strategy

> *"LeetCode is a gym, not a destination. Train smart, not just hard."*

---

## I. THE CURATED LISTS (in order)
1. **[NeetCode](https://neetcode.io) 150** ⭐ — covers all 25 patterns, best free starting point
2. **Blind 75** — the original FAANG-prep list (subset of 150)
3. **LC Top Interview 150** — official curated
4. **LC Top 100 Liked** — popular high-quality problems
5. **Company-tagged** (Premium) — your target company, sorted by frequency (last 6 months)

---

## II. THE METHODOLOGY
For each problem:
1. Attempt for 25-30 min (Medium) / 45 min (Hard)
2. If stuck, read editorial for the IDEA (not the code)
3. Close editorial, re-implement from understanding
4. Note the pattern in your journal
5. Star for re-solving in 7 days if hard

**Never copy-paste solutions.** Re-derive.

---

## III. THE DAILY DIET
- 2-3 problems/day
- 1 below your level (warmup, confidence)
- 1-2 at-or-above your level (growth)
- Mix patterns; don't do 5 DP problems in a row

---

## IV. PREMIUM: WORTH IT?
LeetCode Premium gives:
- Company-tagged problems (huge for targeted prep)
- Frequency sorting (solve most-asked first)
- Solutions + video
- Mock interview features

**Verdict**: worth it for the 2-3 months before interviews.

---

## V. THE DIFFICULTY DISTRIBUTION
- Early prep: 70% Medium, 20% Easy, 10% Hard
- Mid prep: 60% Medium, 40% Hard
- Late prep: company-tagged at all difficulties

---

## VI. TRACKING
Maintain a spreadsheet:
| # | Problem | Difficulty | Pattern | Time | Solved? | Re-solve date |

Review weekly. Patterns you fail repeatedly → drill them.

---

## VII. THE ANTI-PATTERNS (avoid)
- Solving only Easy (comfort zone → no growth)
- Memorizing solutions (fails on variants)
- Reading editorial after 5 min (no struggle → no learning)
- Never re-solving (forgetting)
- Grinding without reflection

---

## VIII. THE NUMBERS
- ~150-300 quality problems (with reflection) > 1000 mindless
- NeetCode 150 + 100 company-tagged = solid FAANG prep
- Quality and reflection > raw count
